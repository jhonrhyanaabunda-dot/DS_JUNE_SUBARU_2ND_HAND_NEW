01The two lanes

What "Subaru 2nd hand" actually means.

"Second hand" simply means the vehicle has had a prior owner. With Subaru, that splits into two clear lanes, and knowing the difference is the whole game.

Get it wrong and you either overpay for coverage you didn't need, or you skip protection you'd have wanted six months down the road. This page was reviewed and posted by the Dalton Subaru National City used-car team, the same crew local drivers have known for years.

Used Subaru

Any pre-owned Subaru on the lot. Priced to move, widest range of years and mileage, and usually the lowest entry point into a Crosstrek, Impreza, Outback, Forester, or Ascent. Coverage is whatever factory warranty time remains, plus whatever the original owner left on the clock.

Subaru Certified Pre-Owned (CPO)

A used Subaru that meets Subaru of America's age and mileage requirements, passes a multi-point factory inspection, and comes with a manufacturer-backed warranty plus added benefits. You pay a bit more than a comparable plain-used unit and get a vehicle Subaru itself stands behind.

Per Subaru of America's published certified pre-owned program details (subaru.com), CPO eligibility is restricted to vehicles within a set model-year window with mileage under a fixed cap, and each one runs through a factory-defined point-by-point inspection before it can wear the certified badge. That is the line between "a used car a dealer looked over" and "a used car the manufacturer is willing to put its own warranty behind."

If you want the lowest sticker, look used. If you want factory-backed peace of mind on your second-hand Subaru, look CPO.

Our team will line both up side by side so the trade-off is obvious before you ever sign.

02Behind the lot

A note on how we stock the used lot.

Every second-hand Subaru that lands on our lot at 2829 National City Blvd gets put through its paces before a price tag goes on it. Here is what that actually looks like behind the scenes, not a marketing promise, but the work itself.

We pull the VIN before we decide what to do with a car

When a trade-in or auction unit comes in, our service team pulls the VIN and runs the vehicle history before we decide what to do with it. We have declined cars over branded titles and unresolved open recalls that the numbers flagged, units that looked clean on the lot but didn't check out on paper. We'd rather wholesale a car than retail a problem to a National City neighbor who's going to bring it back to our service drive.

The Subaru-specific inspection

On the units we do keep, our techs inspect brakes, tires, fluids, and the all-wheel-drive driveline that every modern Subaru runs. For Subaru specifically we check the boxer engine's known wear points, head gaskets and oil consumption on older naturally-aspirated fours, and we verify the AWD system engages cleanly, because a Subaru with a tired center differential is a Subaru that's about to get expensive. On more than one Outback we've replaced worn rear wheel bearings and a leaking valve-cover gasket before the car ever saw a customer, simply because the inspection caught it. We've also walked plenty of units back to wholesale when the repair math didn't favor the buyer.

Here's the catch most buyers get wrong

A lot of shoppers walk in dead-set on certified pre-owned because it sounds safer, then balk at the price gap and leave empty-handed. Here's the honest version: CPO is not always the right call. If you're buying an older, higher-mileage Subaru, say a six- or seven-year-old Outback you plan to drive into the ground, that car usually won't even qualify for certification, and chasing a CPO badge on it makes no sense. In that case a well-inspected used unit with a third-party warranty, or just a smart cash buy, often serves you better than stretching for a certified car you didn't need.

We'd actually steer you away from CPO if the math doesn't work for how long you plan to keep the car. The flip side is also true: if you're financing a newer, low-mileage Subaru and keeping it five-plus years, the manufacturer-backed coverage and added benefits usually pay for themselves the first time something expensive goes wrong. The right answer is about your situation, not about which lane sounds better at the door.

09Used & CPO buyer questions

Frequently asked questions about buying a Subaru 2nd hand.

What's the difference between a used and a Certified Pre-Owned Subaru?

A used Subaru is any pre-owned model on the lot. A Subaru Certified Pre-Owned vehicle has passed Subaru's factory multi-point inspection, meets the brand's age and mileage standards (published at subaru.com), and includes a manufacturer-backed warranty.

CPO costs a little more and adds factory protection. A plain used unit costs less and gives you the widest range of years and mileage.

Should I always buy CPO over a regular used Subaru?

No, and we'll tell you so. If you're buying an older, higher-mileage Subaru to drive for a few years, it likely won't even qualify for certification, and a well-inspected used unit is the smarter buy.

CPO earns its premium when you're keeping a newer, low-mileage car long-term and want factory coverage behind it. The right call depends on your budget and how long you plan to own the car.

Do you inspect used Subarus before they go on sale?

Yes. Our techs inspect brakes, tires, fluids, and the all-wheel-drive driveline on every unit we retail, and we check Subaru-specific wear points like head gaskets, oil consumption, and AWD engagement.

We've replaced worn bearings and leaking gaskets before cars ever reached a customer, and we've declined units outright over branded titles or unresolved recalls.

How do I check a used Subaru's history and recalls myself?

Run the VIN for free at nhtsa.gov to see any open safety recalls, and ask us for the vehicle history report on any unit, we'll show it to you.

We encourage every used buyer to verify the car independently rather than take a dealer's word for it.
